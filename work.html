WEB DESIGN

ChemE Car Project Team

To update and redesign ChemE Car's website to better fit its purposes of attracting both new recruits and new sponsors, I first started with a general sketch and then began implementing code. Cornell ChemE Car is an engineering project team that designs, and constructs several model cars powered soley via chemical reactions. As I transitioned from pure graphics to code, I kept my eyes open for more seamless ways of presenting information, especially for the recuitment page which I changed quite a bit. I stuck to a theme of dark complementary colors as to maintain a general theme as well as to indicate the website's affiliation with Cornell Univserity. The ChemE Car website is hosted on a Cornell Server.

I decided to encorporate a few general questions in accordion style, a new addition to the ChemE Car website that was partially motivated by my own journey through the application and interview phases and partially motivated by commentary from my peers.

When it comes to Cornell Engineering Project Teams, there tends to be quite a bit of confusion over who is ellegible to be a part of one. On top of that, there are other general questions like team culture and level of commitment that are important indicators for a given person of if they have found the right place for them.

WEB DESIGN

Base Productions

As part of a design team for a project-based course, I contributed to building a website that serves as an international hub for BASE, a student-run dance company composed of over 45 members and specializing in a multitude of dance styles. The site has a very prevalent design that is simple yet aesthetically pleasing, has good functionality with a working slideshow, a well put together form and a drop down navigation bar for the mobile site, and lastly it serves the purpose of promoting the team through the website and to have a way to gauge interest from prospective members.

I did the initial responsive design sketches, as well as the implementation of the majority of the work involving images. In addition, I also put time towards designing the responsive menu.

I designed and constructed the javascript-based responsive image gallery. Though slightly different, my experience with Java helped me greatly in problem solving, allowing me to achieve a smooth and responsive gallery.

PRINTS

With the protection of Cape Cod's seals, comes the explosion of the population. And with seals, come the sharks. Massachusetts has had a long, tense, relationship with the animal, one which has brought forth the American classic, Jaws, and countless others which serve to depict the vilified shark as an enemy of the human race. Yet creative fanatics stir continuous fear of the animal and forever questioning of its motives off the screen. The Sharp Truth is a project which serves to combat this perspective, and highlight the truth of the shark. It includes a multitude of sources spanning from 1895 to 2020, from first person narratives to scientific facts, which all come together to cut through the noise and sketch out our conflicting reality.

DATA STRUCTURES

The Visability Project

To visually show what information reaches the American Gen Z through algorithms created by popular platforms, I created the Visibility Project. Using the vis.js library, I designed and built a node network, populating it with self-made graphics meant to depict recognizable interfaces and general information "types", and color-coding them to align with the intensities of visable light to the human eye.

Absolutely no information is free. After leaving many digital wakes, this is something that my generation is learning. Every search in Google, every site visited and trivial quiz taken is used to sort, filter, and present information to us in the future with an agenda. This process results in a sort of virtual bubble, a continuous stream of somewhat similar content that is presented right within our reach—at the expense of older, less “relevant” content being pushed farther, and farther away.
